What is Islam?

Islam is a religion of believeing in One God and his Prophet, Prophet Muhammad (Peace Be Upon Him)

What is a mosque?

A mosque (or mosque in Arabic) is the holy place of worship for Muslims.

What are the five pillars of Islam?

  1. Shahada: Bearing Witness that there is only one God, and that Prophet Muhammad (Peace Be Upon Him).
  2. Salah (Prayers): These are the five daily prayers that are prescribed for every Muslim. These are 5 times to connect with Allah and recite some Quran.
  3. Zakat (Giving charity): - Giving back to those who need it is a big part of Islam. “A man is not a believer who fills his stomach while his neighbour is hungry.” "Charity does not decrease wealth" -the Prophet (PBUH)
  4. Sawm (Fasting): This is the pillar of fasting from sunrise to sunset in the holy Islamic month of Ramadan. It lasts about 29-30 days, depending on the moon. Fun fact: The dates for this year correspond with March 21 - April 22, 2023!
  5. Hajj (Pilgrimage): Every able Muslim must take the pilgrimage to Mecca if they are able to, just as the Prophet did many years ago when he was escaping persecution in Medina. The Ka'aba, in Mecca, is the direction all Muslim's face when they pray their 5 daily salat (prayers).

What are the six pillars of Faith?

  1. Belief in Allah: Allah is the Arabic word for "God". Allah is the only God, has no partners or family, He is alone.
  2. Belief in His Angels: There are over __x___ angels in Islam. Gabriel (Jibreel) is the one who revealed the first verse of the Quran to Prophet Muhammad(PBUH).
  3. Belief in His Books: There have been many scriptures revealed throughout history: The Torah was sent to Mosesm The Gospel was sent to Jesus, the Psalms was sent to David, and the Quran was sent to Muhammad (peace be upon them all).
  4. Belief in His Messengers: There have been many messengers throughout history. Only 25 are mentioned by name in the Quran. These include Adam, Abraham, Moses, Noah, Jesus, Muhammad (May Allah be pleased with all of them).
  5. Belief in The Day of Judgment: Life is a test. On the Day of Judgment, that is when the world ends and we are accounted for our actions.
  6. Belief in Divine Destiny: Everything is written. Everything good or bad that happens is under Allah's will.

What are some of these words I always hear Muslims use?

  • Allah: Literally: "God" in Arabic.
  • In Sha Allah: "God Willing". Used when talking about anything in the future.
  • Masha Allah: Literally: "God Willed It". Used when talking about something good that happened or how someone looks!
  • Allahu Akbar: Literally: God is greatest. Used whenever something good happens, also said at the start of prayer.
  • Bismillah: Literally: "In the name of Allah". Said at the beginning of any task or meal.
  • Astaghfirullah: Literally: "I seek forgiveness from Allah".
  • PBUH / SAW (written): PBUH = Peace be upon him. SAW = Sallallahu Alayhi Wasallum ("PBUH but in Arabic).
